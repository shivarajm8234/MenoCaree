What is Menopause?

Menopause is a natural biological process marking the end of menstrual cycles. It typically occurs in your 40s or 50s.

Key Points:

  • Natural part of aging
  • Average age is 51 years
  • Diagnosed after 12 months without periods
  • Caused by hormonal changes

Introduction

Menopause is a significant phase in a woman's life, marking the end of reproductive years. Understanding menopause can empower women to manage their health better during this transition.

Definition

Menopause is defined as the time that marks the end of your menstrual cycles. It is diagnosed after you have gone 12 months without a menstrual period.

Causes

Menopause occurs when the ovaries stop producing estrogen and progesterone, leading to the cessation of ovulation and menstruation.

Impact

Menopause can affect physical health, emotional well-being, and overall quality of life. Common effects include hot flashes, mood swings, and changes in sexual function.

FAQs

  • What age does menopause typically start? Most women experience menopause between the ages of 45 and 55.
  • Can menopause be delayed? Certain lifestyle factors, such as smoking and body weight, can influence the timing of menopause.
